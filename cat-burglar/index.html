The Metropolitan Museum of Art housed the world's most comprehensive collection of ancient artifacts, housed in a fortress-like building. It housed a Roman signet ring dating to 49 BCE, supposedly worn by Julius Caesar himself. The gold band, no larger than a quarter, bore Caesar's personal seal and represented one of the most significant archaeological discoveries of the century.
